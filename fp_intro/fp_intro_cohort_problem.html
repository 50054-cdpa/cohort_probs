Lambda Calculus

Lambda Expression

The valid syntax of lambda expression is described as the following EBNF

(Lambda Terms)t::=x∣λx.t∣t t\begin{array}{rccl} {\tt (Lambda\ Terms)} & t & ::= & x \mid \lambda x.t \mid t\ t \end{array}(Lambda Terms)​t​::=​x∣λx.t∣t t​

Where

  • xxx denotes a variable,
  • λx.t\lambda x.tλx.t denotes a lambda abstraction.
    • Within a lambda abstraction, xxx is the bound variable (c.f. formal argument of the function)
      and ttt is the body.
  • t tt\ tt t denotes a function application.

Note that given a lambda term, there might be multiple ways of parsing (interpreting) it. For instance,
Given λx.x λy.y\lambda x.x\ \lambda y.yλx.x λy.y, we could interpret it as either

  1. (λx.x) (λy.y)(\lambda x.x)\ (\lambda y.y)(λx.x) (λy.y), or
  2. λx.(x λy.y)\lambda x.(x\ \lambda y.y)λx.(x λy.y)
